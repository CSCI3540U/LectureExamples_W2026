CSRF — Challenge

This challenge is separate from the bank demo. You must craft your own attack from scratch. The target is a different application with a different endpoint and form fields.

Scenario

A user is permanently logged in to an account management portal. Their profile page contains an unprotected form that lets them change their email address. Your goal is to change their email to pwned@csrf.attack without them clicking anything.

The victim's profile is at /csrf/challenge/profile (opens in a new tab so you can inspect it while working).

Steps

  1. Open the victim's profile and inspect the page source — find the form's action URL and the name of the email input field.
  2. Go to the attack editor and write HTML that automatically submits a POST to that endpoint with email=pwned@csrf.attack — without the victim clicking anything.
  3. Click Deploy in the editor. Your HTML is served as a page and loaded in the preview iframe, simulating the victim visiting your evil site.
  4. If the attack works, the victim's email below will change. Then claim your flag.
